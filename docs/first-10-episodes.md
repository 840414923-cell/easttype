## Episode 3 — Chinese Daily Life / Warm Water

**Video Topic:** Why Chinese people drink warm water

**Why this topic today:** Biggest cultural difference. Non-Chinese people are always curious about this. Easy to share. Zero medical claims.

**Hook:** In China, we don't drink ice water. Ever.

**Full Script (EN):**

In China, we don't drink ice water. Ever.

Even in summer. Even at restaurants.

My friends from other countries always ask: "Why?"

One idea from Eastern wellness is this.

Cold water is like putting ice on your stomach.

It slows things down.

Warm water helps things move.

I don't know if it's right.

But my grandma has been doing it for 80 years.

**Full Script (ZH):**

在中国，我们不喝冰水。从来不。

夏天也不。在餐厅也不。

其他国家的朋友总问："为什么？"

中式养生里有一个说法。

冷水就像在胃上放冰。

它让一切变慢。

- "like putting ice on your stomach" — 可以比划一下，像在放冰块
- "80 years" — 微笑，这个结尾要有温度

**Caption:** Why do Chinese people always drink warm water? Even in summer. Even at restaurants. One idea: cold water is like putting ice on your stomach. Warm water helps things move. My grandma has been doing it for 80 years. 🫖

## Episode 8 — Body Signal (Mythbust) / Same Tired, Different Body

**Video Topic:** Same tired, completely different cause

**Hook:** Everyone thinks being tired is just one thing. It's not.

**Full Script (EN):**

Everyone thinks being tired is just one thing. It's not.

In Eastern wellness, tiredness can come from two different places.

One: your body has no charge. You sleep fine but still wake up drained. The fix? Sweet potato and red dates tea. Slow fuel.

Two: your body has no fire. You're cold AND tired. The fix? Ginger and cinnamon tea. Restart the furnace.

Same symptom. Completely different body. Completely different fix.

I'm practicing using English to translate my body. You can tell me if my English is off, but trust the logic.

**Full Script (ZH):**

大家都以为累就一种情况。不是的。

在中式养生里，累可能来自两种不同的原因。

第一种：身体没电了。睡了还是醒不来。怎么办？红薯红枣茶，慢慢充电。

第二种：身体没火了。又冷又累。怎么办？生姜肉桂茶，重新生火。

同一个症状，完全不同的身体，完全不同的解法。

## Launch Notes

**Posting Schedule:** 1 per day, same time daily. Consistency > frequency.

**First 3 days:** Post between 7-9AM in your target timezone (US morning = scroll time).

**After Episode 1:** Pin Episode 1 to profile so new visitors always see who you are first.

**Reply to every comment** in the first 10 days. Every single one. This is how you build community from zero.

**Don't check analytics** for the first 2 weeks. The numbers will be small. That's normal. Focus on consistency.
