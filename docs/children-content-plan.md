## Why This Sprint

**Discovery (2026-07-19):** Pinterest data showed a children's digestion pin massively outperformed general adult-focused pins. The winning pin had a complete structure: symptom checklist + named TCM foods + recipe + timeline + body type CTA. This matches the AEO Citation Template (`docs/aeo-citation-template.md`) almost exactly.

**Strategy:** Open a greenfield children's health vertical that:
1. Zero keyword cannibalization (no existing child pages on site)
2. Maps parents directly to landing pages (vs current "all pins → homepage")
3. Each page has inline body type quiz CTA for conversion
4. Forms a topic cluster for SEO + AEO compounding
5. Targets `chinese medicine for child X` keyword family (4-12K monthly searches each)

**Funnel:**
```
Pinterest pin (parent sees child symptom)
  → Dedicated /wellness/chinese-medicine-for-child-X page
  → Inline quiz CTA ("Discover YOUR body type")
  → /quiz → /result → checkout
```

## Page Template (every page, no exceptions)

Follow `docs/aeo-citation-template.md` strictly:

- **H1**: `[Keyword]: [2-3 TCM Pattern Names] and [Food Category] That May [Verb]`
- **Quick Answer Box** (100-150 words, names 2+ patterns + 3+ specific foods)
- **H2: How Chinese Medicine Views [Symptom]** (200-300 words)
- **H2: [Symptom] Patterns** table (4 cols: Pattern | Key Signs | Mechanism | Food Direction)
- **H2: What Causes [Symptom]** (5 sub-sections)
- **H2: Foods That May Help** table (8-9 rows: Food | TCM Property | How It May Help | How to Prepare)
- **H2: Foods to Limit** (6 bullets)
- **H2: Daily Habits** (7 numbered items)
- **H2: When to See a Doctor** (disclaimer + red flags)
- **FAQ** (4-5 Q&As with FAQPage JSON-LD)
- **Medical disclaimer footer**
- **Inline CTA**: "Discover YOUR body type → /quiz" (mid-page + footer)
- **Target word count**: 1,600-1,900

## Child-Specific Elements (differentiate from adult pages)

Each page MUST include:
- "For children" / "for kids" / "for toddlers" / "for teens" in title and H1
- Smaller portion sizes (half bowl, small cup)
- Gentler herbs (avoid strong ones like ginseng for young kids)
- Parent-facing voice ("your child" not "you")
- Age-appropriate guidance (under 3 vs 3-7 vs 8-12 vs teen)
- "Always consult your pediatrician" disclaimer (more prominent than adult pages)

## Anti-Programmatic Rules (CRITICAL — AGENTS.md §11)

To avoid Google's programmatic content penalty:
- **Every page's Quick Answer must have a unique opening** (no shared templates)
- **Every page's dietary advice must use a different framing angle** (history / mechanism / parent story / food-first / habit-first)
- **Every page's pattern table must use different real-food examples**
- **No two pages share the same FAQ question wording**
- **Each page has a unique case study or example**

## Internal Linking Strategy

Each page must have **3-5 internal links**:
- 2-3 links to related child pages (within the same day's cluster first)
- 1 link to related adult wellness page (e.g., child constipation → /wellness/chinese-medicine-for-constipation)
- 1 link to relevant body type page (`/types/qi_deficient` etc.)
- 1 link to `/quiz` (CTA)

**Cross-cluster linking:**
- Day 1 (Spleen digestion) ↔ Day 6 (Spleen low energy)
- Day 1 (Spleen digestion) ↔ Day 7 (Spleen immunity)
- Day 3 (Lung respiratory) ↔ Day 7 (Lung immunity/allergies)
